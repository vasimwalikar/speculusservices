Medical

The medical field is getting better results and lead times with the help of additive manufacturing. The need to visualise things in physical space, was the need of the industry. With traditional manufacturing techniques, the complex shapes of the human anatomy were impossible to produce. This is the gateway for 3D printing aka additive manufacturing. Architecture

Architecture

The need to visualise an edifice before it is completed has been driving the human imagination from the bygone days. Techniques like , drawing, mapping and model making are the most common to visualise architectural models. But the demand for stronger and models with better longetivitiy have pushed model makers to go in search for modern techniques to make the models. They have found answers with 3D printing, where scale models can be done with pin point accuracy. As the models are plastic, they are easy to paint and have a better strength compared to the traditional start board or balsa wood models.

We are having the potential to produce architectural models from scratch. Many of the models available will not be printable; they need to undergo a process of modification and made compatible for 3D printing. This is a technique that our team has mattered with a combination of softwares. We are able to print structures upto a meter in height.

We use special materials like, laybrick and laywood to provide brick and wood finish for the buildings as required.

Spare parts

Spare parts

Inventory is the biggest challenge for any supply chain. It becomes more challenging, when the parts are getting out dated and new technology is taking over the existing ones. Situations arise when critical parts need to be replaced, without which the functionality of the machine is not possible. In most of the cases, these parts would be available unless the machine is an old one. This calls for a spare part hunt. But who has the time to retrieve the drawings and start hunting for a shop floor just to produce one part?

The ball lands in the court of 3D printing and it is the best solution to provide the part. A single part in string plastic like PETG can be printed, and provide a timely solution. The solution may be not permanent but it will definitely reduce machine down time. Parts can be produced in both metal and plastic as per the patient needs.

Industrial

Industrial

apid prototyping is a very thriving field in the industrial sector. For any new product development, the starting point is a prototype. After the concept is complete, the next step is to get to a working proto. This is the most challenging phase, as the budget allotted would be less at the same time the lead time is very less to prove the design. Prototyping calls for not more than one sample, and most of the manufacturing processes will not stop at one.

3D printing would be the best solution to produce off the shelf samples, the CAD design is being sliced into many layers, and the part is being printed layer by layer until the final part is reached. The best part about this process would be zero material wastage, no tooling required. This is exactly what saves the costing in producing, protoyping by 3D printing.
